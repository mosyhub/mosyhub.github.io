About the Project

This rotoscope animation project involves frame-by-frame editing to isolate and stylize motion. It was done entirely using Adobe After Effects 2023 tools, combining manual brush work, keyframes, effects, and layering techniques.

Tools Used

RotoBrush Tool (After Effects)

Used to isolate the dancing subject from the background using frame-by-frame strokes. This allowed me to insert text or effects behind the subject without affecting the foreground.

Pen Tool (After Effects)

Helpful for creating manual masks on tricky edges that the RotoBrush couldn't detect properly — especially around fast movement areas like hands and feet.

Effects & Presets Panel (After Effects)

Used effects like Fill for recoloring objects, Glow to add emphasis, and Tint for mood shifts. These boosted the stylized look of the background and subject transitions.

Transform Properties + Stopwatch (After Effects)

Used to animate the subject and background — including zoom-in effects, fade transitions, and subtle pans. This made the scene feel more cinematic and alive.

Freeze Feature (After Effects)

Once the roto selection was clean, I used Freeze to lock the frames in place. This prevented After Effects from recalculating and shifting the selection across frames.

Timeline Panel (After Effects)

Coordinated all layers, keyframes, and transitions here. Essential for syncing the motion of the background, text, and subject with the music and visuals.

CapCut

Used for quick mobile edits and final color grading. I also used CapCut to add modern video effects and overlays that were easier to apply here than in AE.

Microsoft Clipchamp

Used during the early phase for organizing clips and testing out pacing. Its drag-and-drop interface helped visualize the structure before jumping into After Effects.
